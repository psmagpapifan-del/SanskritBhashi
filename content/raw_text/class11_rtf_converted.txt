Chapter 1: Vedamrutam (वेवामृतम् - Vedic Nectar)

Source: Rigveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda

| Sanskrit Text (Devanagari)                                   | English Translation                                                                               |
| :----------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **सङ्गच्छध्वं सं वदध्वं सं वो मनांसि जानताम्।**              | Go together, speak together, let your minds be of one accord.                                     |
| **देवा भागं यथा पूर्वे सञ्जानाना उपासते॥**                   | Just as the ancient gods, being of one mind, accepted their portion of the sacrifice.             |
| **समानी व आकूतिः समाना हृदयानि वः।**                         | May your aim be common, may your hearts be united.                                                |
| **समानमस्तु वो मनो यथा वः सुसहासति॥**                        | May your mind be one, so that you may live well together.                                         |
| **यज्जाग्रतो दूरमुदैति दैवं तदु सुप्तस्य तथैवेति।**          | That divine mind which travels far when one is awake, and likewise returns when one sleeps.       |
| **दूरङ्गमं ज्योतिषां ज्योतिरेकं तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु॥** | That light of lights which travels far, may that mind of mine be filled with auspicious thoughts. |

Chapter 2: Paropakaraya Satam Vibhutayah (परोपकाराय सतां विभूतयः)

Source: Jataka Mala by Aryashura

| Sanskrit Text (Devanagari)                                            | English Translation                                                                                     |
| :-------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **बोधिसत्त्वः किल कस्मिंश्चित् नातिमहति... सरसि मत्स्याधिपतिः बभूव।** | The Bodhisattva once became the king of fish in a small lake.                                           |
| **अथ कदाचित्... देवो न ववर्ष।**                                       | Then once, due to the misfortune of living beings, the God (Indra) did not rain.                        |
| **स महात्मा... सत्यतपोबलमेव तेषां रक्षणोपायम् अमन्यत।**               | That Great Soul (the fish king) considered the power of truth and penance as the only way to save them. |
| **अनेन सत्येन सरांसि तोयैरापूरयन् वर्षतु देवराजः॥**                   | By the power of this truth, may the King of Gods (Indra) rain and fill the lakes with water.            |

Chapter 3: Mano Hi Mahatam Dhanam (मानो हि महतां धनम्)

Source: Mahabharata, Udyoga Parva

| Sanskrit Text (Devanagari)                      | English Translation                                                                               |
| :---------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **उत्तिष्ठ हे कापुरुष मा शेष्वैवं पराजितः।**    | Stand up, O coward! Do not lie down here as one who is defeated.                                 |
| **अमित्रा नन्दयन्सर्वान् निर्मानो बन्धुशोकदः॥** | You are delighting your enemies and bringing grief to your kinsmen, being devoid of self-respect. |
| **कुरु सत्त्वं मानं च विद्धि पौरुषमात्मनः।**    | Gather your courage and self-respect, and know your own manhood/valor.                            |

Chapter 5: Aaharavicharah (आहारविचारः - Thoughts on Diet)

Source: Charaka Samhita

| Sanskrit Text (Devanagari)                    | English Translation                                                                |
| :-------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **उष्णमश्नीयात्, उष्णं हि भुज्यमानं स्वदते।** | One should eat warm food, for warm food tastes good.                               |
| **मात्रावदश्नीयात्... केवलं सुखं विपच्यते।**  | One should eat in proper quantity; it digests easily and provides happiness.       |
| **जीर्णेऽश्नीयात्... न चोषमाणमुपहन्ति।**      | Eat only when the previous meal is digested; it does not disturb the bodily fires. |
| **तन्मना भुञ्जीत... न विहसेत्।**              | Eat with a concentrated mind, without talking or laughing excessively.             |

Chapter 10: Sattvamaho Rajastamah (सत्त्वमहो रजस्तमः)

Source: Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 17

| Sanskrit Text (Devanagari)                       | English Translation                                                                                     |
| :----------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **यजन्ते सात्त्विका देवान्यक्षरक्षांसि राजसाः।** | Men of 'Sattva' (purity) worship gods; 'Rajasic' (passionate) men worship spirits/demons.               |
| **अनुद्वेगकरं वाक्यं सत्यं प्रियहितं च यत्।**    | Speech that does not cause agitation, is truthful, pleasant, and beneficial (is the penance of speech). |
| **दातव्यमिति यद्दानं दीयतेऽनुपकारिणे।**          | Charity given with the thought 'it is a duty to give' to one who does no return favor is 'Sattvic'.     |

Chapter 11: Navadravyani (नवद्रव्याणि - The Nine Substances)

Source: Tarkasangraha by Annam Bhatta

| Sanskrit Text (Devanagari)                                      | English Translation                                                                            |
| :-------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **तत्र द्रव्याणि पृथिव्यप्तेजोवाय्वाकाशकालदिगात्ममनांसि नवैव।** | The nine substances (Dravyas) are Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Ether, Time, Space, Soul, and Mind. |
| **गन्धवती पृथिवी। सा द्विविधा नित्याऽनित्या च।**                | Earth is that which possesses smell. It is of two types: eternal and non-eternal.              |
| **शीतस्पर्शवत्य आपः।**                                          | Water is that which has a cold touch.                                                          |
| **उष्णस्पर्शवत्तेजः।**                                          | Fire is that which has a hot touch.                                                            |
